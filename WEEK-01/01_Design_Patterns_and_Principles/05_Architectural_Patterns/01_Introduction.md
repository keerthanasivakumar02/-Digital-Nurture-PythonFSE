# Architectural Design Patterns

## Introduction

Architectural Design Patterns describe the high-level structure of a software application. They provide guidelines for organizing different components, defining their responsibilities, and managing the interactions between them. These patterns help developers build applications that are scalable, maintainable, and easy to modify.

## Design Patterns Included

- Model-View-Controller (MVC)
- Dependency Injection (DI)

## Advantages

- Improves the overall organization of an application
- Separates responsibilities into independent components
- Reduces dependencies between modules
- Makes applications easier to test, maintain, and extend
- Supports the development of scalable software
