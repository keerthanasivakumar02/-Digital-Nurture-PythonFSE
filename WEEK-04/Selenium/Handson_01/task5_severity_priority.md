# Task 5 - Severity vs Priority

## What is Severity?

Severity indicates how serious a defect is and how much it impacts the application's functionality.

It is decided by the QA Engineer or Tester.

### Severity Levels

- Critical
- High
- Medium
- Low

### Example

The application crashes immediately after login.

Severity: Critical

---

## What is Priority?

Priority indicates how quickly a defect should be fixed.

It is usually decided by the Project Manager, Product Owner, or Business Team.

### Priority Levels

- High
- Medium
- Low

### Example

The company logo is missing from the home page before a product launch.

Priority: High

---

## Difference Between Severity and Priority

| Severity | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| Measures the impact of the defect | Measures the urgency of fixing the defect |
| Decided by QA | Decided by Project Manager / Product Owner |
| Related to system functionality | Related to business requirements |
| Technical impact | Business impact |

---

## Severity vs Priority Examples

### Case 1

Issue:
The application crashes during payment.

Severity: Critical

Priority: High

---

### Case 2

Issue:
A spelling mistake on the Home page.

Severity: Low

Priority: High

Reason:
The website is going live tomorrow.

---

### Case 3

Issue:
A rarely used Help page has a broken image.

Severity: Low

Priority: Low

---

### Case 4

Issue:
The Search feature is slow.

Severity: Medium

Priority: Medium
